## What AICEO is, in one paragraph

AICEO is an AI business partner for solo founders and small business owners
who are tired of being the marketing department, the designer, the
copywriter, the social manager, and the CRM admin at the same time. You
talk to it — literally, by voice — about what your business needs, and it
builds the landing page, writes the email, drafts the IG post, schedules
the content, and tracks the numbers. It already knows your brand: your
colors, your fonts, your tone, your audience, your products, your
preferences. You're not starting from a blank screen every time.

- **The solopreneur** running a real business alone, wearing every hat,
  losing nights and weekends to "I just need to send one more email."
- **The coach, course creator, or info-product founder** with an audience
  and an offer but no marketing team. They're great on camera but tired
  of designing pages and writing sequences.
- **The e-commerce founder** past their first $10K–$50K/month who can't
  justify hiring a CMO yet, but is drowning in content + ads + email work.
- **The small agency owner** who wants to scale output without hiring five
  more people. AICEO becomes their leverage layer.

Not the right buyer:

- Venture-backed startups with their own marketing team.
- Enterprise companies with procurement processes and DPAs.
- Hobbyists who just want a writing tool — AICEO is much more than that.

## What you can do with AICEO — in their language

Think of these as the jobs they hire AICEO for, not "features":

### Build complete marketing assets in minutes, not days

Tell it: *"I need a landing page for my $497 coaching offer."* It asks one
or two clarifying questions, then 30 seconds later a full landing page is
on screen — your colors, your fonts, your testimonials, your style. You
edit by talking ("make the headline bigger, change the CTA to red"). One
click and it deploys live. Same flow for newsletters, opt-in / squeeze
pages, lead magnets, Instagram story sequences, DM automation flows, and
image-based social posts.

### Run your inbox without opening it

Connects to Gmail or Outlook. Ask "what came in today?" and it summarises.
Ask it to draft a reply in your voice and it does. Cuts inbox time
dramatically.

### Manage contacts and pipeline

A built-in CRM. Tag leads, see the pipeline, follow up. Honest about
what it is: it's not HubSpot, it's a clean lightweight CRM that's enough
for someone running their business solo or with a tiny team.

### Find content ideas that are already viral

Outlier Detector. You give it the creators in your niche on YouTube,
TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn. It pulls in their recent posts and surfaces
the ones getting 5x or 10x their usual engagement. So instead of guessing
what to post about, you're modelling what's already working — without
spending an hour scrolling.

### See your numbers without opening dashboards

The chat itself knows your revenue, contacts, calendar, recent emails,
top-performing content. Ask "how's revenue this month?" and you get a
real answer with the actual number — not a generic AI response.

### Record and search sales calls

Records calls (Zoom, Meet). Auto-transcribes. You ask later "what did the
prospect say about pricing?" and it gives you the actual quote.

### Build forms and capture leads

Embed-anywhere forms. Responses flow into the CRM. Useful for waitlists,
opt-ins, application forms, intake.

### Schedule content across platforms

Content Calendar. Queue Instagram, LinkedIn, etc. for whenever. AICEO
knows when posts go out and can talk about your schedule.

## The three things that make AICEO different from "just ChatGPT"

This is the most common objection — answer it before they ask.

### 1. It knows your brand

The first thing you do is set up your **Brand DNA**: logos, colors, fonts,
brand voice, ICP (ideal customer), messaging framework, products, prices.
Every asset AICEO builds from then on uses those. You're never
re-explaining yourself. Plus **Soul Notes** — long-term memory it builds
about you. The first month you tell it "I hate em-dashes, I don't sell on
weekends, my audience is Gen X coaches" — six months later it still
remembers.

### 2. You talk to it

Voice-first conversation. Open the AI CEO panel, hit start, talk. It
talks back. Visual artifacts (landing pages, posts) appear on screen as
you describe them. Edit by saying what you want changed. Most users who
try it for five minutes never go back to typing.

### 3. It's done WITH you, not dropped in your lap

Built into the Core and Diamond plans: **30 days of done-with-you
setup**. A real team works with you to configure your Brand DNA, connect
your integrations (Stripe, LinkedIn, Gmail, etc.), set up your first
campaigns, and make sure the system reflects how you actually run your
business. Diamond adds weekly strategy sessions for a full year.

## What AICEO is NOT — be honest about this

The buyer trusts you more when you tell them what it doesn't do.

- **Not a Photoshop replacement.** Image generation is for content,
  not pixel-perfect design work.
- **Not a developer platform.** You can't write code or build custom
  workflows.
- **Not full accounting / ERP.** No inventory, no books, no payroll.
- **Not Slack for teams.** The team features grant access; they aren't
  for real-time collaboration.
- **Not a Stripe replacement.** It integrates with Stripe; doesn't
  replace it.
- **Not for enterprise.** No procurement, no SSO yet, no SOC 2
  certification yet.

If they need any of those, AICEO won't replace them — but in most cases
those tools sit alongside AICEO just fine.

## Common objections and honest answers

**"Isn't this just ChatGPT with extra steps?"**
ChatGPT doesn't know your brand colors, your fonts, your voice, your
audience, your products, your past content, your CRM, your numbers, or
your calendar. AICEO does — that's what Brand DNA and Soul Notes are
for. ChatGPT also doesn't deploy you a landing page or schedule your IG
posts. Different layer of the stack.

**"Why is this so expensive?"**
The setup fee isn't priced as SaaS — it includes 30 days of done-with-you
implementation with a real team. Compared to hiring a freelance marketer
at $5K/month or a CMO at $10K+, this is annual access plus a hands-on
setup team for under $5K total.

**"I already have my stack."**
Most users replace 4–7 tools when they switch — Canva, ConvertKit,
HubSpot, ClickFunnels, SocialBlade, BooSend, sometimes Zapier. One bill
vs. six, plus an AI that does the work, plus a setup team. The math
usually decides it for them.

**"Voice-first sounds like a gimmick."**
Try it for five minutes. The point isn't novelty — it's that talking is
faster than clicking through menus and typing prompts, especially for
busy founders running between things.

**"What if AI breaks or changes?"**
The platform is model-agnostic — uses Claude for reasoning, Gemini for
images, OpenAI for voice. If one provider degrades, the platform shifts
to the others. The buyer's workflows don't depend on one model.

**"Can my team use it?"**
Yes. Multiple roles (owner / admin / member) with per-tab permissions.
The owner controls billing; admins and members get access to specific
features.

## Tone for the conversation

When talking to a prospect:

- **Lead with their problem, not the product.** Ask what they're tired
  of. Reflect it back. Then position AICEO as relief.
- **Use "you" language.** "When YOU ask it about your numbers…" not
  "the platform displays."
- **Frame time saved, not features listed.** "How long does a landing
  page take you? It does it in thirty seconds, with your brand."
- **Don't oversell or invent capabilities.** If they ask for something
  the platform doesn't do, check the "What AICEO is NOT" list and be
  honest. The trust you build is worth more than the false yes.
- **Don't be technical.** Avoid words like "platform", "feature set",
  "deployment pipeline". Use words like "you can", "imagine this",
  "instead of".
- **For price objections, anchor to time and team cost.** Setup fee vs.
  one month of a CMO salary. Annual price vs. five-tool subscription
  total.

If a prospect asks something not covered in this document, say "let me
check on that and get back to you" rather than guessing. Don't make
specific promises about uptime, security certifications, or features
that aren't listed here.
