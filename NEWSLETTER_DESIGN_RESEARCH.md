## INDIVIDUAL BREAKDOWNS

### 1. Alex Hormozi (Acquisition.com / Mozi Money Minute)

**Format:** Hybrid plain-text. Minimal design. Feels like a personal email from a friend, not a marketing blast.

**Visual Design:**
- Near-zero images. No hero images. No inline illustrations.
- White background, black text. Occasionally a single brand-color accent.
- Single-column layout, no sidebars.
- No borders, no colored backgrounds on sections.
- Minimal to no formatting beyond bold text and line breaks.

**Copy Structure:**
- Opens with a single hook line (bold claim, contrarian take, or "I just did X and here's what happened")
- Body uses short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max per paragraph)
- Frameworks: Proof (case studies, before/after), Process (step-by-step playbooks), Philosophy (contrarian takes), Personal (lessons, failures)
- Single CTA at the end, usually a link (not a button)
- P.S. line frequently used as a secondary hook or CTA

**Email SOP (his actual formula):**
1. "I made this for you" (personalization opener)
2. "Here's what it is and how it helps" (value explanation)
3. "Here's how to get it (link)" (single clear CTA)

**Subject Line Patterns:**
- Short (3-7 words)
- Curiosity-driven, often a bold claim
- Examples: "The $100M lesson", "Stop doing this", "I was wrong about..."
- Lowercase preferred, no punctuation tricks

**What Makes It Convert:**
- Feels personal, not promotional
- Value-first approach -- helps, doesn't sell
- Extreme brevity cuts friction
- Single CTA eliminates decision paralysis

### 2. Sam Parr / The Hustle

**Format:** Rich HTML with clean, branded design. News-style multi-section layout.

**Visual Design:**
- Branded header with The Hustle logo
- Single hero image per lead story
- White background, dark text
- Clear section dividers (horizontal rules or thin lines)
- Bulleted lists for "Around The Web" sections
- Moderate image usage (1 hero image, occasional inline images)
- Clean typography with generous white space

**Copy Structure:**
- Table of contents at top (clickable section links)
- Lead story: headline + hero image + 300 words max
- Secondary briefs: 150-250 words each
- "Around The Web": 6 short bullet links to external content
- Total newsletter: under 1,200 words, readable in under 5 minutes

**Headline Patterns:**
- Contrarian: "[Thing everyone thinks is dead] will never die"
- Hidden story: "The wild tale of [unexpected business story]"
- Question mystery: "Why [company/industry] [does unexpected thing]"
- Unexpected business angles from mundane topics

**Tone:**
- Conversational, witty, dry humor
- Sounds like a friend talking, not a journalist reporting
- Pop culture references, memes, witty asides
- Clever but not forced; data-driven but accessible

**What Makes It Convert:**
- Breadth over depth (80% of readers preferred more topics quickly)
- Every word earns its place -- cut ruthlessly
- Subject lines use emoji + engaging description, A/B tested
- The voice/personality is the product

### 4. James Clear (3-2-1 Thursday)

**Format:** Pure plain text. No images. No HTML formatting beyond basic text.

**Visual Design:**
- Zero images, zero design elements
- Plain text email -- looks like a personal email
- No colors, no buttons, no borders
- Only formatting: line breaks and section labels
- White/default background

**Copy Structure:**
- Fixed format every week (never deviates):
  - "3 ideas from me"
  - "2 quotes from others"
  - "1 question for you"
- Total: ~500 words, 2-minute read
- Each "idea" is tweet-sized (1-3 sentences)
- Quotes include author attribution
- Question is open-ended, reflective

**Subject Line Pattern:**
- Always: "3-2-1: [Topic A], [Topic B], and [Topic C]"
- Consistent format builds recognition and habit

**What Makes It Convert:**
- "Most wisdom per word of any newsletter on the web"
- Extreme brevity reduces forwarding friction (readers share constantly)
- Plain text = maximum deliverability, zero spam flags
- Predictable format = zero cognitive load
- The question creates engagement beyond just reading
- 3M+ subscribers proves the model

**Key Insight:** The newsletter's constraint IS its strength. 500 words forces only the best ideas to survive.

- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each, lots of white space)
- Bold headers to break up sections
- Occasional images but primarily text-driven
- Single-column layout

**Copy Structure (APAG Framework):**
- **A = Attention**: Agitate and paint a picture of the problem
- **P = Perspective**: Describe a perspective he sees as wrong and why
- **A = Advantage**: Benefits of seeing it from his perspective
- **G = Gamify**: Steps to overcome the problem + a challenge

**Alternative framework (Introduce/Educate/Simplify):**
- Introduce: What is the problem?
- Educate: Why should they solve it?
- Simplify: How can they solve it?

**Writing Rules:**
- Rule of 3: 3 main points, each with 3 subpoints (flexible)
- Very short paragraphs -- often single sentences as their own paragraph
- Bold text for key phrases and section headers
- Philosophical/existential framing of practical business topics

**What Makes It Convert:**
- One deep idea per week, repurposed everywhere
- The philosophical angle differentiates from pure tactical creators
- Short paragraphs create "waterfall" reading momentum
- APAG framework creates emotional investment before solution

## UNIVERSAL DESIGN RULES (Extracted Patterns)

### Layout
- **ALWAYS single-column layout.** No multi-column except for market data bars.
- **Max width: 600-640px.** This is the industry standard for email rendering.
- **Mobile-first.** Container scales to 100% width on mobile.
- **Generous white space.** Every successful newsletter uses significant padding between sections.

### Typography
- **Body font size: 16-18px.** Never smaller than 14px.
- **Header font size: 22-28px.**
- **Line height: 1.4-1.6** for body text.
- **Web-safe font stack:** `'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif` OR `Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif` for editorial feel.
- **Always include fallback fonts.**
- **Short paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max.** Every top creator does this.

### Color
- **Dominant: White background (#FFFFFF) + dark text (#1a1a1a or #333333).**
- **Accent: One brand color for links, headers, or CTA buttons.**
- **Links: Underlined and colored** (differentiated from body text).
- **Section headers: Can use accent color or ALL CAPS or both.**
- **Dark mode compatible:** Test with inverted colors.

### Images
- **Creator/solopreneur newsletters: Minimal to zero images.** (Hormozi, Clear, Welsh, Koe = near-zero)
- **Media/news newsletters: Strategic images.** (Morning Brew, Hustle = hero images + section images)
- **When using images: max 25% of content area.** Maintain 60% text / 40% image ratio for deliverability.
- **Never use image-only emails.** Spam filters flag them.
- **Hero images: Optional.** Data shows CTA above fold matters more than hero images.

### Spacing
- **Section padding: 24-32px between major sections.**
- **Paragraph spacing: 16px between paragraphs.**
- **Use a spacing scale: 4/8/12/16/24/32px.**
- **Generous padding inside bordered sections: 16-24px.**

### Dividers
- **Thin horizontal rules (1px, light gray #e0e0e0) between major sections.**
- **Bordered sections with rounded corners for news-style newsletters.**
- **Line breaks (not borders) for personal/plain-text style newsletters.**

## COPYWRITING FRAMEWORK RULES

### Opening Hook (First Line)
Choose ONE per newsletter:
1. **Bold Claim:** "Most people get [topic] completely wrong."
2. **Personal Story Entry Point:** "Last Tuesday, I lost $50,000 in 4 hours." (Start at the most engaging moment.)
3. **Surprising Statistic:** "Only 3% of businesses ever reach $1M in revenue."
4. **Direct Question:** "What would you do with an extra $10,000/month?"
5. **Contrarian Take:** "You don't need more leads. You need fewer."

### Body Structure Options

**Option A: Hormozi SOP (Short-form, under 300 words)**
1. Personal opener ("I made this for you")
2. Value explanation ("Here's what it is and how it helps")
3. Single CTA link

**Option B: Justin Welsh Template (Medium-form, 500-800 words)**
1. Identify the challenge
2. Common approaches + why they fail
3. Unique solution in clear steps
4. Brief proof/story
5. CTA

**Option C: Dan Koe APAG (Long-form, 1,500-3,000 words)**
1. Attention: Agitate the problem
2. Perspective: Wrong way vs. right way
3. Advantage: Benefits of the right perspective
4. Gamify: Steps + challenge

**Option D: James Clear 3-2-1 (Ultra-short, under 500 words)**
1. 3 original ideas (tweet-length each)
2. 2 curated quotes with attribution
3. 1 reflective question

**Option E: Morning Brew / Hustle (News-curated, 800-1,200 words)**
1. Market/data bar
2. Lead story (headline + image + 300 words)
3. Secondary briefs (150-250 words each)
4. Curated links section (6 bullets)
5. Sponsored content (marked clearly)

### Paragraph Rules
- **Max 3 sentences per paragraph.** Most top creators use 1-2.
- **One idea per paragraph.**
- **Use line breaks aggressively** -- white space is a feature, not a bug.
- **Bold key phrases** so skimmers get the point.
- **Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items.** Bullet points signal "this is easy to read."

### Closing Rules
- **Single primary CTA.** Never more than 2 CTAs in the entire email.
- **P.S. line is highly effective.** 79-90% of readers read the P.S. Use it for:
  - A secondary CTA
  - A reminder of the main offer
  - A personal aside that humanizes
- **End with value, not a hard sell.**

## SUBJECT LINE RULES

### Proven Formulas
1. **Curiosity Gap:** "The one thing I'd never do again" (can't answer without opening)
2. **Number + Specific:** "3 frameworks that made me $2M" (specificity builds trust)
3. **How-To + Outcome:** "How to write emails that make money"
4. **Contrarian/Unexpected:** "Stop building your email list"
5. **Personal Story Tease:** "I almost quit last week"
6. **Question:** "Are you making this $100K mistake?"
7. **Direct Benefit:** "Your 5-minute business plan"

### Technical Rules
- **Length: 20-40 characters** (45% higher open rates than longer)
- **Personalization increases open rates by 26%**
- **Numbers in subject lines increase open rates**
- **Lowercase preferred** for personal-feeling newsletters
- **No ALL CAPS** (spam trigger)
- **No excessive punctuation** (!!!, ???)
- **Preview text matters** -- treat it as subject line part 2

## CTA RULES

### Placement
- **Primary CTA above the fold** (84% more engagement)
- **Duplicate CTA at end** for readers who scroll
- **P.S. CTA** as a safety net (79-90% read the P.S.)

### Design (for HTML button CTAs)
- **Font size: 16-20px**
- **High contrast:** Dark button + white text OR brand-color button + white text
- **Shape:** Rounded rectangle (4-8px border radius)
- **Padding:** 12-16px vertical, 24-32px horizontal
- **First-person language:** "Get My Free Guide" > "Get Your Free Guide" (20% higher clicks)
- **Action-oriented:** 2-5 words max
- **Tease value:** "Get 3 Free Templates" > "Download"

### For Plain-Text Style
- **Use a text link, not a button.** Underlined, possibly bold.
- **Format:** "Click here to [specific benefit]" or just a raw URL on its own line.

### Conversion Data
- **Single CTA increases clicks by 371%** over multiple CTAs
- **Single CTA increases sales by 1,617%**
- **3+ CTAs have lower click-through than fewer**
- **1-3 sentences above the CTA convert highest**

## TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### Email Container
```
Max width: 600-640px
Background: #FFFFFF
Text color: #1a1a1a or #333333
Link color: Brand accent color (e.g., #2563eb for blue, #dc2626 for red)
Font family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif
```

### Typography Scale
```
H1 (newsletter title): 28-32px, bold, #1a1a1a
H2 (section headers): 22-26px, bold, #1a1a1a or accent color
H3 (subsection headers): 18-20px, bold or semibold
Body text: 16-18px, regular weight, #333333
Caption/meta: 13-14px, #666666 or #888888
Line height: 1.5 for body, 1.2 for headers
```

### Spacing Scale
```
Between major sections: 32px
Between subsections: 24px
Between paragraphs: 16px
Inside bordered sections (padding): 20-24px
Between header and body text: 8-12px
```

### Section Dividers
```
Horizontal rule: 1px solid #e0e0e0
OR
Bordered section: 1px solid #e5e7eb, border-radius: 8px, padding: 20px
OR
Simple line break (for plain-text style)
```

### CTA Button (when using HTML)
```
Background: Brand accent color (high contrast)
Text: #FFFFFF
Font size: 16-18px, bold
Padding: 14px 28px
Border radius: 6px
Width: auto (text-width) or max 300px
Centered in container
```

### Footer
```
Font size: 12-13px
Color: #888888 or #999999
Includes: Unsubscribe link (1-click), company address, social links (icons)
Optional: "Manage Preferences" link (reduces churn vs. just unsubscribe)
Separator: 1px line above footer
Padding top: 32px
```

### Mobile Responsiveness
```
Container: 100% width on screens < 640px
Font size: Same (already optimized at 16-18px)
Images: max-width: 100%, height: auto
CTA button: Full width on mobile
Padding: 16px side padding minimum on mobile
```

## ENCODABLE SYSTEM PROMPT RULES

These are the concrete, actionable rules ready to be encoded into an AI system prompt for generating newsletters:

### FORMAT SELECTION RULES

```
IF newsletter_type == "personal_brand" OR "solopreneur":
    format = "plain_text_hybrid"
    images = "none" or "minimal" (max 1)
    design = "minimal"
    length = 300-800 words

IF newsletter_type == "news_curated" OR "media":
    format = "rich_html"
    images = "hero_image + section_images"
    design = "branded_sections"
    length = 800-1200 words

IF newsletter_type == "wisdom_curated":
    format = "pure_plain_text"
    images = "none"
    design = "zero"
    length = 300-500 words
```

### STRUCTURAL RULES (for AI generation)

1. **Never exceed 3 sentences per paragraph.**
2. **Open with a hook, not a greeting.** No "Hey [name]," or "Happy Tuesday!" -- jump straight into value.
3. **First sentence must be one of:** bold claim, surprising stat, personal story moment, direct question, or contrarian take.
4. **Use bold text for 1-2 key phrases per section** that a skimmer would catch.
5. **Every newsletter must have exactly ONE primary CTA.** No more.
6. **CTA copy must be first-person and action-oriented** ("Get My Guide" not "Download").
7. **Include a P.S. line** with either a secondary CTA, personal aside, or reminder.
8. **Section headers must be clear and scannable.** Use bold, ALL CAPS, or accent color.
9. **Bullet points for any list of 3+ items.**
10. **Total word count guidelines:**
    - Quick-hit/value-bomb: 200-400 words
    - Standard personal brand: 500-800 words
    - Deep-dive/thought leadership: 1,500-3,000 words
    - News digest: 800-1,200 words
11. **Subject line: 20-40 characters, curiosity-driven, include a number when possible.**
12. **Preview text: Treat as subject line part 2.** Must add context, not repeat.
13. **Footer: Minimal.** Unsubscribe link, company address, optional social icons. No clutter.
14. **Never use more than one image in a personal brand newsletter.**
15. **For news-style newsletters: use ALL CAPS section headers with accent color.**
16. **Line height: 1.5. Font size: 16-18px body. Max width: 600px.**
17. **60% text / 40% image maximum ratio** for deliverability.
18. **Always include a plain-text version** (multipart MIME) for deliverability.

### TONE RULES (by newsletter archetype)

```
IF archetype == "authority" (Hormozi style):
    tone = "direct, confident, no-BS, value-packed"
    voice = "mentor who's done it and is sharing the playbook"
    avoid = "hedging language, passive voice, fluff"

IF archetype == "witty_news" (Brew/Hustle style):
    tone = "conversational, clever, dry humor, pop-culture-aware"
    voice = "smart friend explaining what happened today"
    avoid = "corporate speak, jargon without explanation, being try-hard funny"

IF archetype == "wisdom" (Clear style):
    tone = "thoughtful, concise, philosophical, reflective"
    voice = "wise teacher distilling complex ideas into simple truths"
    avoid = "verbosity, tactical how-to's, selling"

IF archetype == "growth" (Bloom/Welsh style):
    tone = "curious, actionable, empathetic, structured"
    voice = "successful peer sharing frameworks that worked"
    avoid = "bragging without teaching, theory without action"

IF archetype == "philosophical_practical" (Koe style):
    tone = "existential, contrarian, deep-then-practical"
    voice = "philosopher who also builds businesses"
    avoid = "surface-level advice, pure motivation, generic tips"

IF archetype == "contrarian" (Sanchez style):
    tone = "snarky, opinionated, provocative, entertaining"
    voice = "successful investor letting you in on secrets"
    avoid = "being safe, conventional advice, boring"
```
